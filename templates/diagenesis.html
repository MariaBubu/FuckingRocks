What is Diagenesis?

Diagenesis is the sum of all physical, chemical, and biological changes that occur to a sediment after its initial deposition, but before metamorphism. It's the engine that turns loose sand into sandstone, and biological shells into solid rock.

In the context of fossils—like the marine shells you're studying—diagenesis is the process that allows them to survive for millions of years. When a creature dies, its shell is often made of Aragonite or chaotic biological Calcium Carbonate. Over time, pressure and mineral-rich groundwater force the chaotic atoms to rearrange into a highly ordered, stable crystal lattice.

As this process hits 100%, the chaotic micro-structure fully locks into the macroscopic Trigonal Crystal System of Calcite. At this stage, the fossil has fundamentally transformed into a gemstone-like state, preserving the ancient creature's shape in solid crystal for eternity.
